This is the standard cross-domain email authentication pattern. The client's IT team will publish records on **resustainability.com** that delegate sending authority to your infrastructure. You don't need DNS access to their domain — they handle it. You just need to give them the right records to publish.

## What records the client needs

To send `From: visitor@resustainability.com` and pass SPF/DKIM/DMARC, the client must add these to **resustainability.com** DNS:

1. **SPF** — A TXT record at `resustainability.com` that includes your sending service's SPF mechanism. Example: `v=spf1 include:_spf.<provider>.com ~all`
2. **DKIM** — A CNAME (or TXT) record at a selector subdomain like `lovable._domainkey.resustainability.com` pointing to your sending service's DKIM key. The provider generates the public key; the client just publishes the CNAME.
3. **DMARC** (optional but client may ask) — A TXT at `_dmarc.resustainability.com`.
4. **Return-Path / MAIL FROM alignment** — sometimes a CNAME like `mail.resustainability.com` → provider's bounce host.

**These records can only be generated by the email-sending service you use.** Gmail SMTP cannot generate them for `resustainability.com` — only Google Workspace on that exact domain can. So we need to switch to a service that supports **third-party domain authentication**.

## Recommended path: Lovable Email (built on Resend)

Resend supports adding `resustainability.com` as a sending domain on your account. It generates the exact SPF + DKIM + DMARC records the client needs to publish, and once verified, you can send `From: visitor@resustainability.com`.

You already have `RESEND_API_KEY` in secrets, and Lovable has built-in email infrastructure that wraps this cleanly.
